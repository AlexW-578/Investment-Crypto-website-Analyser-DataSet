What is a cookie?

Cookies are small text files that many websites automatically download to a user's computer or other device when they browse online. Cookies are primarily used to collect standard Internet log information and website visitor behavior data. This information is typically used to track a visitor’s use of a website, improve the functionality of the website, and to compile statistical reports on website activity.

Cookies, for example, allow a website to recognize a user’s device. Cookies also allow you to navigate between pages efficiently, remember preferences, and generally improve the website user experience for you. They can also be used to tailor advertising to your interests through tracking your browsing across websites.

Cookies are one of two variants. Session cookies are deleted automatically when you close your browser and persistent cookies remain on your device after the browser is closed (for example to remember your user preferences when you return to the website). Persistent cookies, also known as permanent cookies, are cookies that are stored on your hard drive until they expire or until you delete the cookie.

Cookie Categories

Fullstory may deploy essential cookies, functional cookies, analytics cookies, or targeting or advertising cookies on its Site.

Essential cookies. These cookies are necessary in order to enable you to move around the Site and use its features. Without these cookies, services you have asked for (such as navigating between pages) may not be provided. We also use cookies which are strictly necessary to allow you to access our Site, to move between pages and to receive the services which you have requested. The types of data collected by these required cookies may include: session identifier, IP address, webpage URL, referring website, and security tokens.

Performance cookies. We may make use of analytics or performance cookies to analyze how our visitors use our Site and to monitor our Site’s performance. This allows us to provide a high-quality experience by customizing our offering and quickly identifying and fixing any issues that arise. Performance cookies are also used by services provided by third parties on the Site. For example, we might use performance cookies to keep track of which pages are most popular, which method of linking between pages is most effective, and to determine why some pages are receiving error messages. We might also use these cookies to highlight articles or Site services that we think will be of interest to you based on your usage of the Site. The information collected by these cookies is not associated with the personal information you manually provide to us when you use the Site (such as name and contact information).

Functional cookies. We may make use of cookies to provide you with certain functionality. For example, to remember choices you make (such as your user name, language or the region you are in), or to recognize the platform from which you access the Site, and to provide enhanced and more personal features. This allows us to provide a high-quality experience by customizing our offering and quickly identifying and fixing any issues that arise. For example, we might use functional cookies to keep track of which method of linking between pages is most effective and to determine why some pages are receiving error messages.

Targeted advertising cookies. Advertising cookies (or targeted advertising cookies) collect information about the browsing habits associated with your device and are used to make advertising more relevant to you and your interests. They are also used by services provided by third parties on the Site, such as ‘Like’ or ‘Share’ buttons in addition to providing the requested functionality. Third parties provide these services in return for recognizing that you (or more accurately your device has) have visited a certain website. These third parties put down advertising cookies both when you visit our Site and when you use their services and navigate away from our Site.

Also, our emails may contain a single, campaign-unique “web beacon pixel” to tell us whether our emails are opened and verify any clicks through to links within the email. We may use this information to determine for example which of our emails are more interesting to you.
