Information We Automatically Collect

We automatically collect information about your interactions with our Services, including information about the device you use to access the Service.

Cookies and Device Identifiers. A cookie is a small file placed on your device when you visit a website that can be understood by the website that issued the cookie. We use cookies and other technologies, including third-party device identifiers, to collect information and support certain features of the Site. For example, we may use these technologies to:

  • collect information about how visitors use the Services (for example, which pages you visit, which links they use, and how long they stay on each page);
  • support the features and functionality of the Services (for example, to save a visitor the trouble of re-entering information already in our database or to prompt the settings you established on previous visits);
  • personalize a visitor's experience when they use the Services; and
  • improve our marketing efforts, including targeted advertising.

We may use both session cookies and persistent cookies. A session cookie expires when you close your browser. A persistent cookie remains on your hard drive for an extended period of time. The information we collect using cookies and similar technologies may or may not be directly identifying of you, but we may link it to Personal Information that you provide. If you do not wish to receive cookies, you may set your browser to reject cookies or to alert you when a cookie is placed on your computer. Although you are not required to accept cookies when you use our Services, you may be unable to use all of the functionality of the Services if your browser rejects cookies. Please note that due to technical constraints of browser cookies, we cannot retain your opt out after you have cleared your browser's cookies, or if your browser blocks first or third-party cookies. The Services use Cookies which do not allow for any type of control over your device and do not install programs on your device. The Services do not have any access or control over Cookies or other measurement technology used by third parties accessible by the Services and, albeit we undertake the best effort to inform our partners cannot ensure the compliance of third parties with laws on privacy. Many browsers allow you to control and to disable Cookies via their settings. Please be aware that disabling technical Cookies may preclude or worsen a user's experience of the Services. For more information, please visit:

  • http://www.allaboutcookies.org or
  • www.youronlinechoices.com

  • Compliance with applicable laws or performance of a contract:  In specific circumstances, we may need to process your Personal Information to comply with a relevant law/regulation or to fulfill our obligations under a contract to which you are subject. Where we process your Personal Information to meet our legal obligations, you will likely not be permitted to object to this processing activity, but you will usually have the right to access or review this information unless it would impede our legal obligations. Where we are processing your Personal Information to fulfill our obligations under a contract to which you are a party (e.g., to provide you with our products and services), you might not be able to object to this processing, or if you do choose to opt-out or object to our processing, it may impact our ability to perform a contractual obligation that you are owed.
