What are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that enable us and our service providers to uniquely identify your browser or device for various purposes, including those described below. Cookies are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and to improve user experiences. Cookies are also used to provide information to the owners and operators of websites.

First party cookies are cookies that are placed by us.

Third party cookies are created and set by third parties.

How Long do Cookies Last?

Cookies may last for different periods of time depending on why the cookies are used:

  • Session cookies are temporary cookies that are stored until you leave the website. Session cookies cannot be used to track your browser or device from website to website.
  • Persistent cookies last for a fixed period of time, as defined within the cookie, and will allow a website or its service providers to recognize the device when the user of the device returns to the website that placed the cookie.

The cookies we place on your device will be removed when you delete your account with us.

How to Manage Cookies

You can decide whether to accept or reject non-essential cookies by adjusting your cookie settings using Cookie Settings. You can change your mind and change your consent choices at any time.

You may also adjust your cookie preferences by changing the settings on your browser. You can usually find these settings in the “options” or “preferences” menu of your browser. As the means by which you can refuse cookies through your web browser controls vary from browser-to-browser, you should visit your browser’s help menu for more information. For example, instructions on how to manage cookies on Google Chrome are available here.
