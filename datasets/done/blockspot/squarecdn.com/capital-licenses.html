Commercial Programs:

Square Credit Card

Square Capital, LLC (“Square Capital”) acts as a service provider to Celtic Bank, a FDIC-insured, Utah chartered bank. Celtic Bank issues Square Credit Card, an open-end, revolving small business commercial credit account, to small business customers by leveraging the efficiencies of Square Capital’s existing technology platform and infrastructure for the marketing, onboarding and servicing of such credit cards. Celtic Bank is the lender and originating creditor for the program under the agreement and does so using its own funds. Square Capital, as a service provider to Celtic Bank, provides marketing and advertising services, application processing, cardholder documentation, and post-funding customer service. Square Capital purchases the receivables from an extension of credit issued by Celtic Bank on an account on the settlement day.

All potential commercial cardholders start as existing Block customers that use the Block Point-of-Sale system. Such customers are provided an opportunity to access credit originated by Celtic Bank.

Paycheck Protection Program (PPP)

SBA PPP

PPP Loans were originated by Celtic Bank, a Utah-Chartered Industrial Bank, Member FDIC, located in Salt Lake City, UT and were available through Square Capital from April 27, 2020 to July 8, 2020. Loan term is 5 years, with 1% annual interest assessed on any amounts that are not forgiven after the deferral period. The deferral period varies based on the date of forgiveness, but is a maximum of 16 months from origination. In order to receive a first round PPP loan, a business was required to establish, among other things, that they were in operation as of February 15, 2020. More information can be found on the SBA website here.

PPP Loans were underwritten and issued by Square Capital, LLC, a PPP lender licensed by the SBA and were available through Square Capital from January 29, 2021 to May 31, 2021. Loan term is 5 years, with 1% annual interest assessed on any amounts that are not forgiven after the deferral period. The deferral period varies based on the date of forgiveness, but is a maximum of 16 months from origination. In order to receive a second draw loan, a business must establish, among other things, that they are still in business, they were in operation as of February 15, 2020, had 25% decreased revenue in 2020 relative to 2019, and have used or will use their first draw PPP loan for authorized purposes. In order to receive a first draw loan, a business must establish, among other things, that they are still in business, and they were in operation as of February 15, 2020. More information can be found on the SBA website here.

Square Installments

Loans Issued by Celtic Bank*

*At this time, Square Installments is not available.

Loans issued by Celtic Bank, a Utah-Chartered Industrial Bank, Member FDIC, located in Salt Lake City, UT. Borrowers can choose to pay over 3, 6, or 12 months with fixed interest rates from 0-30%.* The interest rate and annual percentage rate (APR) are the same. The interest rate may vary based on credit history and other factors. Our 0% rate is available for qualifying applicants who select autopay and choose a 3-month term. Select Square businesses may offer 0% APR for longer terms based on individual credit.

A reduced APR is available when automatic payments are selected during the application process. APR is the only cost associated with Square Installments—no prepayment fees, deferred interest, or service fees. The interest rate never changes throughout the loan term. The product does not charge compound interest (interest charged on interest). Interest is only charged on the amount originally financed.

Square Capital, LLC loans issued prior to July 9, 2019

Square Capital charges interest based on the loan amount and duration. Prospective borrowers have the option of selecting a loan term of 3, 6, or 12 months. Interest is calculated based on a fixed monthly rate (the advertised annual rate divided by 12) and is assessed daily against the loan’s outstanding principal balance.
