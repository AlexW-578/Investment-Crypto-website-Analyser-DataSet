The Online Channels may provide links to other third-party websites and features. Some of these third-party websites may be co-branded with Cloud Software Group even though they are not owned, controlled, operated, or maintained by Cloud Software Group. Unless otherwise disclosed in this Privacy Statement or other supplementary privacy notice provided to you, Cloud Software Group does not automatically share your personal information with those third-party websites and is not responsible for their privacy practices. These websites are subject to their respective privacy policies. In some cases, we may provide the Offerings jointly with other businesses. For these co-branded offerings in which a third party is involved in your transactions, we will sometimes share or jointly collect customer data–which may include personal information–related to those transactions with that third party.

References in this Privacy Statement to “Cloud Software Group” are references to the Cloud Software Group entity responsible for the processing of your personal information, which generally is the Cloud Software Group entity that collects your personal information.

This Privacy Statement does not apply to personal information:

  • processed on behalf of our customers when providing Cloud, technical support, and consulting services, which is described in the Cloud Software Group Data Processing Addendum and related product documentation.  Customer end-users should direct any questions about our customers’ privacy practices to our customer who collected their information.

Cookies and Other Similar Technologies

Cloud Software Group uses cookies, web beacons (including pixels and tags), and similar technologies on our Online Channels which may read or write information on your device in order to collect certain information about you by automated means. A “cookie” is a text file that websites send to a visitor’s computer or other Internet-connected device to uniquely identify the visitor’s browser or to store information or settings in the browser. A “web beacon,” also known as an Internet tag, pixel tag or clear GIF, links web pages to web servers and their cookies and may be used to transmit information collected through cookies back to a web server.

We use these automated technologies to collect information about your device or equipment, browsing actions, and usage patterns. The information we obtain in this manner includes IP addresses and other identifiers associated with your devices including Apple Advertising Identifier or Android Advertising ID, types of devices connected to our Offerings, device characteristics (such as operating system), language preferences, referring/exit pages, navigation paths, access times, email open rates, browser preferences and characteristics, installed plugins, local time zones, local storage preferences, clickstream data, and other information about your online activities.

We use on our Online Channels both first-party cookies (served directly by our website domain when you visit our Online Channels) and third-party cookies (served by a third-party website when you visit our Online Channels and certain third-party websites with whom we have partnered). Some of these cookies are session cookies (which are automatically deleted when you close your browser) and others are persistent cookies (which remain on your computer or other Internet-connected device for a period of time after you end your browsing session, unless you delete them).

The cookies we use on our Online Channels include: strictly necessary cookies, functional cookies, analytics cookies, and targeting/advertising cookies, as described below.

Strictly Necessary Cookies

We use strictly necessary cookies on our Online Channels that are necessary for us to provide you with our products and services. These include cookies used to securely authenticate you to our website and identify you after you have logged in and user-centric security cookies used to increase the security of the products and services we provide to you (such as to detect authentication abuses). Given the necessary functionality of these cookies, they typically do not require consent.

Functional Cookies

These cookies allow us to analyze your use of the Online Channels to evaluate and improve our performance. They may also be used to provide a better customer experience on the Online Channels. For example, remembering your log-in details, saving what is in your shopping cart, or providing us information about how our site is used.

Analytics Cookies

We use analytics cookies to collect information on how users navigate and use our Online Channels, such as how the users traverse our Online Channels, the pages they view, how long they stay on a page and whether the page is displayed correctly or whether errors occur. Such cookies help us to improve the performance of our Online Channels and make the Online Channels more user-friendly. These cookies are provided by third-party analytics providers, including Google Analytics. To learn more about Google Analytics and how to opt out, please visit Google Analytics. To learn more about these analytics services and how to opt out, please view the Cookie Preferences link in the bottom of the page for the Cloud Software Group business you are interested in managing analytics cookies for.

Advertising Cookies

Advertising cookies are used to control and manage online advertisements. These cookies may collect information on whether a user has reacted to an advertisement and visited another website. Such cookies help us offer you content and provide targeted information that is more relevant to your interests. They also help us to measure the effectiveness of our advertisements and communications. In the EU, we deploy analytics and advertising cookies on our websites only when users have opted-in as required by applicable law.

Your browser may tell you how to be notified when you receive certain types of cookies or how to restrict or disable certain types of cookies. Please note, however, that without cookies you may not be able to use all of the features of our Online Channels. For mobile devices, you can manage how your device and browser share certain device data by adjusting the privacy and security settings on your mobile device.

Additional information about our cookie practices and how to manage your cookie preferences is available in the Cookie Preferences link in the bottom of the page for the Cloud Software Group business you are interested in managing cookie preferences for. To the extent required by applicable law, we will obtain your consent before using cookies or similar tools.

The providers of third-party apps, tools, widgets, and plug-ins on our Online Channels (such as Twitter, LinkedIn and Facebook) also may use automated means to collect information regarding your interactions with these features. This information is collected directly by the providers of the features and is subject to the privacy policies or notices of these providers. To the extent permitted by applicable law, Cloud Software Group is not responsible for these providers’ information practices.

Global Privacy Control (GPC)

Cloud Software Group recognizes Global Privacy Control (“GPC”) signals where legally required. GPC is a browser setting that a user can set in order to send a signal to websites visited regarding the user’s cookie preferences. If your browser or browser extension has GPC enabled, we will recognize that signal and opt you out of non-essential cookies. For more information about GPC and how to implement GPC opt-out preference signals, please visit globalprivacycontrol.org.
