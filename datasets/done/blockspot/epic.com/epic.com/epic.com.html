How Nursing Voices Drive Meaningful Change

May 05, 2025
Spartanburg Regional Healthcare System improved nurse satisfaction and efficiency by involving nursing leaders and front-line staff in EHR decision-making. Governance changes led to time-saving improvements such as turning on flowsheet macros to fill in standard values with one click and device integration to reduce manual documentation, saving 9,000 hours annually. Spartanburg Regional now ranks in the top 25% of Epic organizations for nursing documentation time.

Boosting Organ Donor Registrations Through MyChart to Save Lives 

April 14, 2025
ACT Health added a link in MyChart to the national Australian Organ Donor Register to help people document their donation wishes. Nearly 12,300 people clicked the link in 2024, making MyChart among the most-used methods to bring people to the website across the country. The conversion rate—the percentage of people who then signed up to become a donor—was 30%, compared to 8% for other recruitment methods.

Closing the Malnutrition Diagnosis Gap: Effective Strategies for Better Identification, Documentation, and Treatment

April 07, 2025
Malnutrition leads to higher mortality and readmission rates, and while up to 50% of hospitalized adults in the U.S. are malnourished, only 5-8% are diagnosed. UI Health Care, UW Health, and MaineHealth implemented documentation improvements and fostered cross-team collaboration to improve malnutrition diagnosis and treatment in their hospitals. Through more accurate identification of malnutrition in admitted patients, two organizations saw an 8-12% increase in diagnosis rates and one reported $650,000 in additional annual reimbursement to cover the increased cost of care for malnourished patients. Their efforts serve as a model for reporting to CMS on the Global Malnutrition Composite Score.
