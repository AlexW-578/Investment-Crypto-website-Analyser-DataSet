India and Pakistan: A Troubled History

Hostilities between India and Pakistan escalated this week after India launched strikes on several sites in Pakistan and Pakistan-administered Kashmir. Relations between the two neighbors, both nuclear powers, deteriorated rapidly after 26 people were killed by gunmen in India-administered Kashmir last month. India has long accused Pakistan of supporting insurgent groups in the disputed Kashmir region, a charge that Pakistan denies. The countries’ fraught relationship began in 1947, when British India was partitioned into India and Pakistan. The Hindu ruler of Muslim-majority Kashmir controversially acceded to India in October 1947, prompting the first of four wars between the newly independent countries—three of them directly over Kashmir.

Victory in Europe

Eighty years ago today the European phase of World War II ended with the unconditional surrender of all Nazi German forces to the Allies. The event became known as Victory in Europe (V-E) Day (pictured below, New York City, May 8, 1945). Here’s more on the run-up to that day, and the following final months of the war.

The end of Nazi Germany

By April 25, 1945, Soviet forces had Berlin surrounded. When it was clear that Germany had lost the war, Adolf Hitler died by suicide on April 30, with advancing Soviet troops less than half a mile away. German forces in northwestern Europe formally surrendered soon after, on May 4. This was followed by a more expansive surrender, covering all German forces, with the document-signing ceremony at Gen. Dwight D. Eisenhower’s headquarters at Reims, France, in the presence of Soviet, U.S., British, and French delegations. At midnight on May 8, the war in Europe was officially over.

V-J Day

As Nazi Germany fell, the war in the Pacific continued. After the U.S. dropped atomic bombs on Hiroshima (August 6) and Nagasaki (August 9)—and the Soviet Union formally declared war against Japan—the Japanese government agreed to surrender on August 10. After negotiating some terms of the surrender, emperor Hirohito formalized the agreement and announced it to the citizens of Japan the following day, August 15, which is now known as Victory in Japan Day.
