What is Forex

Forex, short for foreign exchange, is the global marketplace where currencies are traded. It operates as a decentralized market, meaning transactions occur directly between parties, usually through brokers or financial institutions, rather than a centralized exchange. The Forex market is the largest and most liquid financial market in the world, with a daily trading volume exceeding $6 trillion.

Currency pairs, such as the EUR/USD (Euro to US Dollar), GBP/JPY (British Pound to Japanese Yen), and USD/JPY (US Dollar to Japanese Yen), are the most commonly traded assets. The goal of Forex trading is to speculate on the price movements of these currency pairs, aiming to profit from changes in exchange rates.
